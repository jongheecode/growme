For GrowMe (and likely other visual/UI work with this user), the user creates final visual designs themselves in another tool and brings them in (this is how the `html/` mockups arrived in the first place). They said explicitly, when confirming the home-screen mockup during brainstorming: colors/UI in what I show them are placeholder, and when a screen actually needs a real design, they want **a prompt describing what design is needed**, not a finished visual from me.

**Why:** They want to author the actual look themselves (likely in an external design/prompt-based tool), using my brief as the input.

**How to apply:** During brainstorming/planning, it's fine to use quick structural mockups (via the visual-companion skill) to validate layout/interaction/flow — that was welcomed and worked well (see [[project_v2_app_pivot]] home screen decision). But when implementation reaches a point that needs a finished visual design (colors, illustration, polished component styling), don't invent it — write a short design-brief/prompt (what screen, what elements, what mood/constraints, what data it shows) and hand it to the user instead of producing final CSS/visual polish myself.
